Rethink Sustainability 

Every choice we make on this planet will impact the environment.

The environment allows us to breathe without worrying as we are all doing our part.

What if I were to tell you that the way we currently recycle is inefficient?

How most people Recycle

Obtaining a Product

People are looking for products that are either convenient, affordable or can fulfill their expectations based on advertising and word-of-mouth.

Using a Product

While certain products can be useful, these tend to be one-use products. Some can have multiple uses, yet every product has to go somewhere.

Wish Cycling

After a product is used, people can make assumptions about how to recycle it and not be entirely sure. Certain products need to be recycled in a specific way. Not only this, different regions process recycling and garbage differently.

Continuing the Cycle

As more and more products are used daily, the amount of garbage that can be prevented from entering our landfills can make a difference in our environment.

Time to Re-Think How Recycle


We need to approach Recycling differently by focusing on the Psychology behind our actions.

By focusing on 5 key components, you can know how to recycle better.

The 5 Key Components of 


Organizing Materials

It's easy to forget that at one point, the common items that you use were in a different state during their lifecycle. A lot of the products that are used and consumed are a combination of various materials.

By understanding the composition of the everyday products that we use, we can determine how to recycle these products.

Separation of Materials for Disposal

When we find solutions to the best way to dispose of the materials in our everyday products, it becomes easier to turn a difficult problem into a more manageable one. 
Based on the product, users can create entries of how to dispose of each object.

Location Matters

Did you know that the location of where you dispose of an object may differ between regions?
Based on the various facilities, population density, and current practices; municipalities may dispose of objects differently.

RePyschle gives you the option to include a geographic location of where the entry is based to give others an idea of what is available in their current area. 

Understanding Patterns

By using the RePsychle Database, the entries that you make and the ones that others may create can give a better understanding of how to recycle more efficiently.

An example of this is understanding the various EcoScore that is submitted with the object. If the EcoScore is high, then this is an object that should be used when available.

Seeing various database entries can encourage more thoughtful behavior. 

Build a Community

Engaging with others using Social Media and various Internet Platforms can give you the opportunity to brainstorm for the next big idea on how to tackle this crisis!

Learning from others and sharing knowledge is one of the many ways to help with Recycling!
